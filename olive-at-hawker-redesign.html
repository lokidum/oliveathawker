Our Story

Three Decades of
Family Hospitality

Olive at Hawker is the latest chapter in the Yianoulakis family story, one that stretches back over 30 years in Canberra's hospitality scene. It started with John and Aleka at the award-winning Hellenic Trattoria in Woden, where their commitment to authentic Mediterranean cooking earned them a loyal following. When their sons George, Dimitri, and Arthur took the reins and opened Olive at Mawson, that same philosophy carried over. Then Belconnen locals kept asking when they'd get their own, so the brothers did what felt natural. They crossed the lake.

The Hawker location was a gutted dry cleaner and video store. It took nearly a year of after-hours shifts and early mornings to transform it into the 100-seat restaurant it is today. Head chef Steve Muscat, who's been cooking alongside the family for over 12 years, runs the kitchen. And Aleka, the family matriarch, keeps everything and everyone together, the way she always has. The food is seasonal, made entirely on site, and rooted in the Greek and Italian traditions the family grew up with.

"From John and Aleka's Hellenic Trattoria to the next generation at Olive. Over 30 years of Greek hospitality in Canberra, from our family to yours."
