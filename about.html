Our Story

Three Decades of
Family Hospitality

From the Hellenic Trattoria in Woden to Olive at Hawker — a family's unwavering commitment to authentic Mediterranean cooking.

Est. 1994 Canberra, ACT Greek & Mediterranean Family Owned

In 1994, George and Dimitri Yianoulakis opened the doors of the Hellenic Trattoria on Hindmarsh Drive in Woden. It was a modest place — warm lighting, hand-painted menus, and the quiet hum of their mother's lamb slowly roasting in the kitchen out the back. The family had carried with them something no restaurant guide could quantify: a way of cooking that came from memory, from island villages, from the belief that feeding people well is an act of love.

Over the years, the restaurant became woven into the fabric of Canberra. Regulars came for the moussaka and stayed for the company. Wedding parties filled the dining room with noise and laughter. Sunday lunches stretched long into the afternoon, with extra bread brought out without being asked. The kitchen kept no secrets — every recipe was honest, rooted in the same sun-dried landscape that the family had left behind.

"We built this place on the food our grandparents cooked for us. Every plate that leaves our kitchen carries that."

The Yianoulakis Family

Today, Olive at Hawker continues that tradition under the careful eye of head chef Steve Muscat and the Yianoulakis family. Everything is made on site — from the pastry folded over spanakopita to the slow-roasted lamb shoulder that has appeared on every menu since 1994. The kitchen uses the finest local produce, seasonal ingredients, and recipes that have been shaped by three decades of devotion.

Olive is not a restaurant that chases trends. It is a place built on the conviction that good food, shared with people you love, is more than enough. That has always been the Yianoulakis philosophy. It always will be.
